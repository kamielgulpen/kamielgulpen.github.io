Hi, I'm Kamiel — a PhD student in Computational Science trying to understand how societies tip from one state to another. I study the mechanisms behind social tipping points and how we can simulate these critical transitions to inform better policy decisions.

My research sits at the intersection of complex systems, network science, and social dynamics. I'm working at RIVM (National Institute for Public Health and the Environment) and the University of Amsterdam, where I develop agent-based models and computational tools to explore how small changes in social systems can cascade into large-scale transformations.

How can we use the idea of social tipping points for policy, and how can we simulate this?

I'm particularly interested in the feedback loops between individual behavior, social influence, and collective outcomes — and in designing interventions that can help societies navigate toward positive tipping points. When I'm not modeling social systems or debugging code, I'm probably exploring Amsterdam by bike or discussing the latest developments in complexity science.

Research Interests

  • Social tipping points and critical transitions
  • Agent-based modeling and computational social science
  • Network dynamics and social influence
  • Complex systems for policy and decision-making
  • Simulation methods for social phenomena
